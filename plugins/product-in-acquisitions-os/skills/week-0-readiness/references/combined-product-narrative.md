# Combined Product Narrative (One Page)

A one-page narrative that Product, Sales, and Success can all repeat without improvising. This is the source language for every customer conversation in the first 90 days.

## The five answers

### 1. What is the combined product, in one sentence?

[One sentence. No buzzwords. A customer-facing person should be able to deliver it on a sales call without changing a word.]

### 2. Who is it for?

[Primary buyer persona. Secondary persona if relevant. Be specific about company size, role, and the problem they own.]

### 3. What can a customer do now that they could not before?

[The new capability the combination enables. Frame it as a customer outcome, not a feature list. If you cannot answer this in two sentences, the deal thesis is not yet sharp enough.]

### 4. What does this product explicitly not do?

[The does-not boundaries. Naming what is out of scope is what makes the in-scope answer credible. Be direct about products or features being sunset or deprioritized.]

### 5. What is the 3-year horizon?

[Where the combined product is going. One paragraph. Should be ambitious enough to attract talent and grounded enough that engineers and PMs can sequence work toward it.]

## Repeat-without-improvising test

Read the narrative out loud. Then ask:

- Could a new account executive deliver the answer to question 1 on a customer call today?
- Could a customer success manager handle a "what changes for me?" question using only this language?
- Could an engineer on the acquired team explain to a friend at another company what they are building, using these words?

If any answer is no, revise.
