# Product Integration Owner Charter

**Acquisition**: [Acquirer] acquires [Acquired]
**Close date**: [Date]
**Charter owner**: [Name, Title]
**Effective**: Pre-close through Day 90

## Mandate

The Product Integration Owner is accountable for one outcome: by Day 90, the combined product team is stronger for having merged. This means a single roadmap, a single positioning narrative, customer commitments honored or renegotiated transparently, and acquired product talent with real ownership and influence.

## Authority

The Product Integration Owner has decision authority over:

- Product roadmap prioritization and sequencing for the combined product
- Scope decisions (what stays, what sunsets, what merges)
- Customer commitment disposition (honor, renegotiate, sunset with notice)
- Pre-close and post-close cross-team product communications
- Resource allocation across the combined product organization for the first 90 days

Decisions outside this scope (pricing structure, M&A financial terms, legal, public press) are escalated to the executive sponsor named below.

## Time commitment

Minimum 60 percent dedicated time for the first 90 days. Direct reports manage day-to-day product work outside the integration scope.

## Reporting line

Reports directly to [Executive Sponsor, Title], who is accountable to the board or CEO for the deal thesis.

## Success measures

| Horizon | Measure |
|---------|---------|
| Day 30 | Decision rights documented, combined narrative shipped to GTM, customer inventory complete |
| Day 60 | Merged roadmap published, sales enablement live, top 20 accounts contacted by senior leadership |
| Day 90 | Acquired team has named ownership of meaningful scope, retention risk review complete, combined team participates in normal planning cycles |

## Escalation path

Conflict resolution sequence:

1. Product Integration Owner attempts resolution with named decision owners
2. If unresolved within 48 hours, escalate to Executive Sponsor
3. If unresolved within 72 hours of executive escalation, escalate to CEO
