# Customer Commitment Inventory

The single most underbuilt asset at close. Every promise made to a customer (in a contract, sales call, support ticket, or public statement) needs an owner, a disposition, and a communication plan.

## How to use

1. Assign a single owner to drive completion within 7 days
2. Pull from CRM, support tickets, signed contracts, recent sales call recordings, and public statements
3. For each commitment, decide: honor, renegotiate, or sunset with notice
4. Match each disposition to a customer-facing communication owner and date

## Disposition guidance

**Honor**: The commitment is feasible under the new product strategy and will be delivered as promised. Communicate to the customer in the first two weeks that nothing changes.

**Renegotiate**: The commitment is partially feasible or the timing needs to shift. Senior product or sales leader (not a CSM) opens the conversation. Offer a concrete alternative, not an apology.

**Sunset with notice**: The commitment will not be delivered. Provide as much lead time as the contract requires plus a buffer. Be direct about why. Offer a path forward, even if the path is "here is a competitor we recommend."

## Risk callouts

| Risk type | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Top-20 account with material commitment at risk | Senior product or GTM leader contacts in week 1 |
| Public statement that may be hard to honor | Comms partner drafts revised public position |
| Pattern of similar commitments across many customers | Treat as a single product decision, not many individual conversations |

## Completion criteria

Inventory is complete when:

- Every customer in the top 20 has every commitment logged
- Every commitment has a disposition and an owner
- Every honor and renegotiate disposition has a communication date scheduled
- Every sunset disposition has been reviewed by legal and a comms partner
