## Category definitions

**V (Vision)**: The two teams disagree on what the product should become in three years. Cannot be resolved through negotiation. Leadership must decide and communicate. If unresolved by Day 30, this is the single highest-risk item in the integration.

**Seq (Sequencing)**: Both teams agree on destination but disagree on order. Use customer signals, support volume, and revenue concentration to resolve. The process of resolving this is itself an act of team building.

**Scope**: One side believes a piece of the product should sunset, merge, or change form. Be direct about what is changing, why, and what it means for the people who built it. Ambiguity is not kindness here.

## Resolution protocol

1. Categorize the conflict
2. Assign a decision owner from the decision rights one-pager
3. Gather customer evidence (support tickets, Success notes, sales call recordings, revenue concentration)
4. Hold the resolution conversation in the relevant session (Diagnosis for vision, Decision for sequencing and scope)
5. Document the resolution and the rationale
6. Communicate the decision with context, not spin
