# Session 1: Roadmap Readout

The first joint roadmap session sets the tone for everything that follows. The single biggest mistake is the acquirer presenting first or presenting at all.

## Purpose

Both teams present their roadmaps. Leaders listen as students. Goal is shared understanding of both products and how customers experience them. Not assessment. Not judgment.

## Attendees

- Both PM teams (full)
- Both EM leads
- Both Product leadership
- Acquirer Sales and Success leads (listening only)
- Product Integration Owner (facilitating)

## Pre-session prep

- Both teams send their current roadmap doc 48 hours before
- Both teams prepare a 30-minute walkthrough of their own roadmap, structured as: themes, current quarter, next quarter, 12-month horizon, why this order
- Acquirer team is explicitly told they will not present in this session
- Acquirer leaders are coached to ask clarifying questions, not assessing questions

## Agenda (90 minutes)

| Time | Activity | Owner |
|------|----------|-------|
| 0:00 to 0:05 | Frame the session: shared understanding, not assessment | Integration Owner |
| 0:05 to 0:35 | Acquired team presents their roadmap | Acquired Product lead |
| 0:35 to 0:50 | Clarifying questions only (no opinions) | All |
| 0:50 to 1:20 | Acquirer team presents their roadmap | Acquirer Product lead |
| 1:20 to 1:30 | Closing: name the three biggest themes that overlap, the three biggest places they diverge | Integration Owner |

## Facilitation script for the opening

> "We are going to spend the next 90 minutes building shared understanding of both roadmaps. We are not making decisions today. We are not assessing. The goal is for everyone in this room to leave able to describe both products as the team that built them sees them. Acquired team, you go first. Acquirer team, your job is to listen and ask questions that help you understand, not questions that signal a position. We will get to decisions in the next two sessions."

## Anti-patterns to call out in real time

- "That sounds like what we already do" → redirect to a clarifying question
- "We tried that and it didn't work" → save for the next session
- Anyone from the acquirer offering a recommendation → redirect to listening
- Acquired team going quiet → pause and ask a specific PM to walk through their part

## Output

- Conflict log seeded with the three biggest themes that overlap and the three biggest divergences
- Both teams have a baseline of mutual understanding
- Trust signal sent: the acquirer treated the acquired roadmap as a record of judgment
