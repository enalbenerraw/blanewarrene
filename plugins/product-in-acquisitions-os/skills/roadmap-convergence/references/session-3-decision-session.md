# Session 3: Decision Session

After conflicts are categorized and Vision conflicts are decided by leadership, this session resolves the Sequencing and Scope conflicts using customer evidence.

## Purpose

Make the merged roadmap real. Resolve every Sequencing and Scope conflict with named decisions, named owners, and a path to communication.

## Attendees

- Both PM teams
- Both EM leads
- Both Product leadership
- Customer Success and Support leads (data presenters)
- Product Integration Owner (facilitating)
- Rotate facilitation: if previous sessions were facilitated by the acquirer, an acquired-side leader runs this one

## Pre-session prep

- Vision conflicts decided and documented
- Customer evidence pack: support volume by feature area, revenue concentration by feature usage, named-account roadmap requests by quarter, churn signals by product surface
- Conflict log filtered to Sequencing and Scope conflicts only

## Agenda (3 hours)

| Time | Activity | Owner |
|------|----------|-------|
| 0:00 to 0:10 | Frame: evidence-led, both teams interpret together | Facilitator |
| 0:10 to 0:40 | Customer evidence walkthrough | Success / Support leads |
| 0:40 to 0:55 | Break |
| 0:55 to 1:55 | Walk Sequencing conflicts. For each: present evidence, both teams interpret, decide order | Both teams |
| 1:55 to 2:10 | Break |
| 2:10 to 2:50 | Walk Scope conflicts. For each: name the decision (sunset, merge, retain), name the owner, name the communication date | Both teams |
| 2:50 to 3:00 | Confirm merged roadmap structure and 30/60/90 outcomes | Facilitator |

## Decision protocol

For each conflict:

1. Present the evidence
2. Both teams interpret the evidence in their own words
3. Propose a decision
4. Test the decision: does it serve the customer outcome named in the Vision answer?
5. Name the decision, the owner, and the communication date
6. Move to the next conflict

If a conflict cannot be decided in 15 minutes, escalate per the decision rights one-pager. Do not let one conflict consume the session.

## Anti-patterns to call out

- Decisions made in side conversations during the break
- Acquirer leader making the call before the acquired team has interpreted the evidence
- "We will figure out the communication later"
- Sunset decisions delivered in passive voice

## Visible-influence moment

In this session, ensure at least one decision is made where the acquired team's interpretation of the evidence changed the outcome. Name it out loud: "We are doing this because [acquired team member] surfaced X." It costs nothing. It builds enormous trust when true.

## Output

- Every Sequencing and Scope conflict resolved
- Merged roadmap draft (next 90 days plus 12-month horizon)
- 30/60/90 plan with named owners and measurable outcomes
- Communication plan for any sunset or scope reduction
