# Session 2: Conflict Diagnosis

Once both teams understand each other's roadmaps, diagnose the conflicts before trying to resolve them. The single biggest mistake is jumping to resolution before agreeing on the category of conflict.

## Purpose

Categorize every conflict in the log as Vision, Sequencing, or Scope. Surface the Vision conflicts so leadership can decide them outside this room.

## Attendees

- Both PM teams
- Both EM leads
- Both Product leadership
- Executive sponsor (joining for the Vision conflicts portion)
- Product Integration Owner (facilitating)

## Pre-session prep

- Conflict log populated from the readout session
- Both teams flag any conflicts they think are Vision
- Customer evidence pack: top 10 support tickets per product, top 5 revenue accounts per product, recent named-account roadmap requests

## Agenda (2 hours)

| Time | Activity | Owner |
|------|----------|-------|
| 0:00 to 0:10 | Frame the categories: Vision / Sequencing / Scope | Integration Owner |
| 0:10 to 0:50 | Walk the conflict log, categorize each row | Both teams |
| 0:50 to 1:00 | Break |
| 1:00 to 1:40 | Discuss the Vision conflicts: define the 3-year product, customer problem, does-not boundaries | Leadership + both teams |
| 1:40 to 2:00 | Decide which Sequencing and Scope conflicts will be resolved in the Decision session | Integration Owner |

## Facilitation script for the Vision conversation

> "We are going to answer three questions. What is this product in three years? What customer problem does it own? What does it not do? We will not solve every conflict today. We will name the answer to those three questions clearly enough that the rest of the conflicts become solvable."

## Anti-patterns to call out

- Treating a Vision conflict as Sequencing because it is uncomfortable to decide
- Trying to resolve everything in one session
- Letting a strong-voiced PM dominate the categorization
- Acquirer leaders deciding before the acquired team has spoken

## Output

- Every conflict in the log categorized
- A draft answer to "what is this product in three years"
- A draft answer to "what customer problem does it own"
- A draft answer to "what does it not do"
- Clear ownership of each Vision conflict for leadership decision
- Sequencing and Scope conflicts queued for the Decision session
