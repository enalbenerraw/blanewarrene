# Decision Map Session (30 minutes, Weeks 1 to 2)

A short, structured conversation with each acquired product leader and PM in the first two weeks. The point is to remove the most expensive form of friction: not knowing what you are allowed to decide.

## Attendees

- One acquired product leader or PM
- Their direct manager from the acquirer
- (Optional) Product Integration Owner for the first three sessions to set the pattern

## Pre-session prep

- The decision rights one-pager from `week-0-readiness` is shared with the participant 24 hours before
- The participant is invited to flag any decisions they are unclear about

## Agenda (30 minutes)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0:00 to 0:05 | Frame: this is a decision map, not an evaluation |
| 0:05 to 0:15 | Walk the four questions with the participant |
| 0:15 to 0:25 | Document the participant's specific scope for the next 30, 60, 90 days |
| 0:25 to 0:30 | Confirm follow-up: written summary back to the participant within 48 hours |

## The four questions

### 1. Which decisions do you own?

Walk through the participant's actual scope. Be specific. Not "you own product strategy" but "you own the prioritization decision for the [feature area] roadmap, with input from [people], and you communicate the decision to [people]."

### 2. Which decisions are shared, and with whom?

Name the shared decisions and the named partners. Be explicit about how shared decisions resolve. "We decide together; if we disagree, we escalate to [person]."

### 3. How are conflicts resolved?

Walk through the conflict resolution protocol from the decision rights one-pager. Make sure the participant knows the named escalation owner and the timeline.

### 4. What is your scope for the next 30, 60, 90 days?

Concrete, named scope. Not just "you're responsible for X." A specific outcome the participant owns and will be measured against. Each horizon has at least one named outcome.

## Follow-up

Within 48 hours, the manager sends the participant a written summary:

> "Confirming our conversation. Your scope is [specific]. Decisions you own: [list]. Decisions shared with [named people]: [list]. Conflicts resolve via [escalation path]. 30-day outcome: [named]. 60-day outcome: [named]. 90-day outcome: [named]. Push back if any of this is wrong."

Pushback is welcomed. The summary is a contract, not a directive.

## Anti-patterns

- Treating the session as an onboarding orientation
- Using the session to evaluate the acquired leader
- Manager filling in the participant's scope before asking
- Vague scope ("you'll figure it out as you go")
