# Mentor Pairing Matrix

Pair mentors by function, not seniority. Design rotations for acquired product leaders to shadow cross-functional roles. Mentoring is most valuable when structured around questions, not instructions.

## Pairing principles

- Function to function: product to product, product marketing to product marketing, EM to EM
- Approximate seniority match, not strict
- Mentor is not the mentee's direct manager
- Both sides commit to weekly 30-minute syncs for the first 8 weeks
- Mentor is coached on coaching questions, not instructions

## Cross-functional shadowing rotation

Acquired product leaders rotate through 60-minute shadowing sessions with adjacent functions in the first 4 weeks.

| Function to shadow | Owner on the acquirer side | Suggested topic | When |
|-------------------|----------------------------|-----------------|------|
| Customer Success | | "What are customers asking us this week?" | Week 1 |
| Support | | "What are the top 5 escalations and what do they tell us?" | Week 2 |
| Sales enablement | | "How do we currently equip reps?" | Week 3 |
| Marketing | | "How do we currently position the product?" | Week 4 |

## Mentor coaching brief (give to every mentor)

> "You are not here to teach our way of doing things. You are here to help your mentee navigate the new environment. The most valuable thing you can do is ask questions that help them surface their own perspective and judgment. Sample questions: What outcome are you trying to create? What would you do if you were certain you had the authority? What did your former team do well that you would want to bring with you? Listen more than you talk."

## Pairing review

At week 4 and week 8, both mentor and mentee complete a 5-minute survey:

- Is the pairing useful?
- What is one thing that would make it more useful?
- Should the pairing continue past week 8?

Adjust based on the responses. Failed pairings get re-matched, not abandoned.
