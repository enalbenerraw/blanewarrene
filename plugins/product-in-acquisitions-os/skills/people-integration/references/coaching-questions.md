# 1:1 Coaching Question Library

Build these into 1:1 meetings and leadership stand-ups from Day 1. The questions communicate that the acquired leader's perspective is wanted, not tolerated.

## Outcome questions

- What outcome are you trying to create?
- What would success look like in 30 days?
- What is the one decision that would unblock the most work right now?

## Assumption questions

- What assumptions are you making?
- What would have to be true for this to fail?
- What do you think the acquirer believes that is actually wrong?

## Authority questions

- What would you do if you were certain you had the authority?
- What is the smallest decision you would make differently if it were entirely yours?
- Where are you waiting for permission you do not actually need?

## Perspective questions

- What is one thing the acquirer does differently that you would change if you could?
- What is one thing your former company did that we should adopt?
- What are you noticing that we are not seeing because we are too close to it?

## Trust questions

- What is one thing you have not said in a meeting that you should have?
- What is the question I should be asking you that I am not?
- Is there anyone here you do not trust enough to disagree with publicly? What would change that?

## Energy questions

- What part of your work is energizing right now?
- What is draining you?
- If you imagine yourself six months from now, are you still here? What would have to change to make the answer a confident yes?

## Cadence

- Direct manager 1:1s use 1 to 2 of these questions per week, rotating across categories
- Skip-level 1:1s use the trust and perspective questions
- Quarterly review uses the full set as a structured reflection

## Rules of use

- Listen more than you talk
- Do not respond with a defense of how things are done
- If the answer surfaces something hard, name it. Do not paper over it.
- Follow up on what you hear. Coaching questions without follow-up become a signal that the acquired leader's perspective does not actually matter.
