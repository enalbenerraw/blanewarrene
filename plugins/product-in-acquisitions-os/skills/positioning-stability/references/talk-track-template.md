# Talk Track

The 90-second version of the story. For use in customer calls, sales meetings, conferences, and panel introductions.

## Structure

### The setup (15 seconds)

What changed and why it matters to the customer. One sentence on the acquisition. One sentence on what it unlocks for the customer.

> "[Acquirer] acquired [acquired] in [month]. For our customers, this means [single most relevant customer outcome]."

### The substance (60 seconds)

What the combined product does for the customer. Three points. Each point is a customer outcome, not a feature.

> "Three things customers should know. First, [customer outcome 1]. Second, [customer outcome 2]. Third, [customer outcome 3]. The thing that makes this combination work is [the why behind the deal thesis, in customer language]."

### The next step (15 seconds)

What the customer should do or expect. Concrete and time-bound.

> "Over the next [horizon], you can expect [specific concrete change]. If you have questions, [named contact] is your direct line. The first thing I would suggest is [specific action]."

## Delivery rules

- Read it out loud. If it does not sound like something you would say to a friend, rewrite it.
- 90 seconds means 90 seconds. If it runs longer, cut.
- No buzzwords. No "synergy," "unlock," "leverage."
- Name a person at the end. The customer remembers the name.

## Variants

Build short variants for:

- Email introduction (3 sentences)
- LinkedIn post announcing the deal (4 sentences plus 1 customer outcome)
- Conference panel intro (30 seconds)
- Inbound press inquiry (1 paragraph plus a quote)
