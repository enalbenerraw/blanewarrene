# Top 20 Outreach Plan

Get senior product and GTM leaders on calls with the top 20 accounts on both sides in the first two weeks. The point is not to sell. The point is to listen and demonstrate that the relationship matters.

## Talking points (not a script)

Open with one sentence acknowledging the change. Two sentences naming what you know about the customer's situation specifically. Then ask three open questions and listen.

### Suggested questions

- What concerns do you have that we should know about?
- What is working in the relationship that we should not change?
- What would tell you, six months from now, that this acquisition was good for you?

### What not to say

- "Nothing is going to change" (almost certainly not true)
- "We are excited about the future" (the customer wants to hear about their future, not yours)
- "Let me tell you about our roadmap" (later, after they have spoken)

## Special care for acquired customers

Acquired customers decided to buy from a company that no longer exists in the form it was when they bought it. Acknowledge that directly, without spin. A direct acknowledgment builds more trust than any letter from the CEO about the exciting things ahead.

> "You bought from [acquired] when [acquired] was a standalone company. That has changed, and I want to make sure the relationship still works for you. What should we know?"

## Follow-up commitments

Every call ends with a specific follow-up commitment from the senior leader. Not "we will be in touch." A named action with a date.
