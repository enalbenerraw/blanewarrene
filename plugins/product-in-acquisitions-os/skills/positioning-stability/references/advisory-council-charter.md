# Advisory Council Charter (First 90 Days)

A lightweight council of 8 to 12 customers (mix of acquirer and acquired) who become your most reliable source of positive market signals during the integration.

## Purpose

- Ground the integration in customer reality, not internal noise
- Build a network of customer advocates the company can lean on through the transition
- Surface market reactions early so positioning and roadmap adjustments are made fast

## Membership criteria

- 4 to 6 acquirer customers, 4 to 6 acquired customers
- Mix of company size and industry
- Includes at least 2 customers who have privately expressed concerns about the deal
- Includes at least 2 customers who have publicly supported the deal
- Each member is sponsored by a senior leader on our side

## Cadence

- Monthly, 60 minutes, for the first 90 days
- Quarterly thereafter (transitions to standard CAB rhythm at Day 90)
- Plus one async channel (private Slack or shared doc) for between-meeting input

## Commitments from the company

- A senior leader from product or GTM attends every meeting
- The merged roadmap is shared with this group before public release
- Specific concerns raised by the council are answered within 7 days
- Members get early access to changes that affect them

## Commitments from members

- Attendance at the monthly call
- Honest input, including concerns
- Permission to be cited (anonymously by default, by name if they opt in) in customer reference conversations
- Confidentiality on roadmap content shared in advance

## Agenda template

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0:00 to 0:05 | Welcome, what changed since last meeting |
| 0:05 to 0:25 | Roadmap update with member input |
| 0:25 to 0:45 | Open discussion: what are you hearing in the market about us? |
| 0:45 to 0:55 | Single ask we have for the council this month |
| 0:55 to 1:00 | Close, confirm follow-up actions |

## Output

Each meeting produces:

- A short readout shared internally
- A list of follow-up actions with owners and dates
- A summary of market signals to feed into positioning revisions
