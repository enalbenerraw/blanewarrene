# Sales FAQ

The 15 questions sales reps will hear in the first 60 days. Answers must use the language of the positioning statement.

## Product continuity

### Is the [acquired product] going away?

[Direct answer. If it stays, say so and name the timeline. If it sunsets, say so and name the path. Do not hedge.]

### What happens to [feature X] you promised me?

[Reference the customer commitment inventory. Name the disposition: honor, renegotiate, sunset.]

### Will my data be migrated?

[Direct answer. Timeline and ownership.]

## Pricing and commercial

### Will my pricing change?

[Direct. If yes, when, by how much, and why. If no, say so without caveats.]

### What happens at renewal?

[Direct answer. What stays, what changes, what is up for negotiation.]

### Should I wait to renew until things settle down?

[Direct. Make the case for renewing now if it is true. Acknowledge the question is reasonable.]

## Account and support

### Will my account team change?

[Direct. Name the named contact if you can. Acknowledge the relationship matters.]

### Will support quality change?

[Direct. Reference SLAs. Acknowledge that acquired customers often see this question as the most important one.]

### Who do I escalate to if something goes wrong?

[Named contact. Direct line.]

## Roadmap and integration

### How does this compare to [competitor]?

[Use the competitive response script. Three sentences, grounded, no defensiveness.]

### Are you going to integrate [acquirer] and [acquired] products?

[Reference the merged roadmap. Be specific about what and when. Do not promise more than the merged roadmap supports.]

### Will you still build [feature on roadmap]?

[Reference the merged roadmap and the customer commitment inventory.]

## Strategic

### What does this acquisition mean for your company strategy?

[Use the deal thesis in plain English. The customer is not asking for the financial logic. They are asking whether the company is healthier or weaker.]

### Are layoffs coming?

[Direct. If you cannot answer, say so and name when you will be able to.]

### Why should I trust this is going to work?

[Reference the customer commitment inventory, the merged roadmap, and the visible work being done. Show, do not tell.]
