# Competitive Response Scripts

When a competitor uses the acquisition against you, the response must be direct, grounded, and brief. Three sentences max. No defensiveness.

## The five most likely plays

### 1. "They were acquired and the product will languish."

> "The opposite is happening. Investment in [acquired product] is up [specific], because [the deal thesis in plain English]. Here is what is shipping in the next 90 days: [specific deliverable]."

### 2. "They got rid of the founders and the vision is gone."

> "[Founder name(s)] is [current role]. [Acquired team leader] is [current role]. The combined product roadmap was built with both teams. Here is the next 90 days of that roadmap: [specific]."

If the founders did leave, do not pretend otherwise. Use this version:

> "[Founder] transitioned out of the operating role. The team that built [acquired product] is intact and is [specific scope]. Here is what is shipping in the next 90 days: [specific deliverable]."

### 3. "Pricing is going up, you should switch to us."

> "Pricing on existing contracts is unchanged through [date]. New contracts are priced at [specific]. The reason switching costs more than staying is [specific value], which is in our [customer commitment inventory] for every existing customer."

### 4. "Their roadmap is now controlled by [acquirer], who has different priorities."

> "The merged roadmap was built by both product teams using customer evidence: [specific signal]. The next 90 days deliver [specific]. Here is the public commitment: [specific]."

### 5. "Acquisitions always lead to layoffs, support quality is going to suffer."

> "Support staffing is [specific number] today, the same as 90 days ago. SLAs are [specific], which we publish at [link]. If support quality slips, [named senior leader] is the escalation line."

## Rules of engagement

- Do not repeat the competitor's framing
- Do not use "actually"
- Name a number, a date, or a person in every response
- If you cannot, the response is not ready. Find the number, date, or person.
- Never speculate about the competitor's product. Stay on your customer's outcome.
