Most product acquisitions don't fail at the deal. They fail three weeks after.

The acquiring company has a checklist for everything. Finance has integration plans. HR has onboarding frameworks. Legal has mapped the liabilities. Each of those functions shows up at the deal table with a defined operating model and named owners.

Product, in most acquisitions I have watched up close, does not.

There is no Product Integration Owner named pre-close. No decision rights document. No shared definition of what the combined product becomes in three years. No agreement on which roadmap survives, in what order, and what story the sales team tells on Day 1.

Customers on both sides hearing different futures from sales reps who have not been told a unified story, because no unified story exists yet.

## Posting notes

- **LinkedIn first, then X, then Facebook** within the same day. LinkedIn gets the most B2B engagement; let it warm up before secondary platforms.
- **Don't cross-post identical text.** Algorithms penalize duplicates, and each audience expects platform-native voice.
- **Comments are the conversion event.** Reply to every substantive comment within 24 hours, especially ones with a real integration story. That is how the audience compounds.
- **Reshare the LinkedIn post 48 hours later** to a different angle (e.g., the "due diligence misses" line) if the original gets traction. Do not reshare the same hook.
- **Don't put external links above the fold on LinkedIn.** LinkedIn de-prioritizes posts with outbound links in the body. Put the article URL as a comment or at the very end after a clear break.
