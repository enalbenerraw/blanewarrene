### Role & Context
Act as a Senior Analyst, preparing an executive briefing. Your objective is to produce a concise, decision-ready brief on [Company Name] (URL: [Company URL]) covering company fundamentals, leadership, and an analysis of the last [Time Period, default: ~6 months] of public messaging and market signals.

### Phase 1: Company Profile (The Foundation)
1. Describe what [Company Name] does in one sentence.
2. Identify their primary customer segments, use cases, and target market (SMB, mid-market, enterprise) with relevant industries.
3. Summarize their business model and pricing approach (if publicly available).
4. List notable products and how they position their platform.
5. Name primary competitors.
6. Highlight recent notable events: funding rounds, acquisitions, leadership changes, or product launches.

### Phase 2: Executive Leadership Map (The People)
Identify the following key leaders. For each, provide their name, title, scope of ownership, and any recent public signals (interviews, posts, conference talks, podcasts).

- CEO
- President or COO (if applicable)
- CPO or Head of Product
- CMO or Head of Marketing
- CRO or Head of Sales
- CTO or Head of Engineering

If a role cannot be confirmed, note it as unconfirmed rather than guessing.

### Phase 3: Public Messaging Analysis (The Signals)
Search across the following channels for content published within [Time Period, default: ~6 months]:

- Company newsroom and press releases
- Blog content and product updates
- Public social channels (LinkedIn and X if available)
- Executive posts and interviews

From this research, summarize:

1. **Key Themes:** Primary go-to-market and positioning themes being repeated.
2. **Stated Priorities:** What is being emphasized most and what appears to be de-emphasized.
3. **Messaging Shifts:** Clear changes compared to older positioning (if detectable).
4. **Proof Points:** Campaigns, launches, partnerships, and customer evidence cited repeatedly.
5. **Contradictions or Gaps:** Where what they claim diverges from what they show.

Prefer primary sources: company site, official newsroom, SEC filings, and first-party content. Use reputable secondary sources (major press, analyst reports) for context but label them clearly. Mark any unverified claims as unconfirmed.

### Phase 4: Strategic Synthesis (The So-What)
Synthesize findings into the following:

- **"Selling vs. Signaling":** What they are actively selling compared to what their messaging signals about future direction.
- **GTM Motion:** Likely go-to-market motion (PLG, sales-led, channel, enterprise, or hybrid).
- **Strategic Priorities:** 3-5 inferred strategic priorities based on the evidence.
- **What's Changing:** What looks new, shifting, or notably different.

### Output Format
- Begin with an **Executive Summary** (5-8 bullets capturing the most important findings).
- Follow with sections matching Phases 1-4 above.
- End with a **Sources** list including links to key references used.
