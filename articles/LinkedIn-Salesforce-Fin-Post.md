# LinkedIn: Salesforce / Fin Acquisition

*Drafted 2026-06-17. Aligns with the Thursday "Prioritization Override" and weekend "Routing Discipline" Substack pieces. Soft CTA to Product in Acquisitions OS.*

---

Salesforce just paid $3.6B for an AI agent. Writing the check was the easy part.

On June 15, Salesforce signed a definitive agreement to acquire Fin (formerly Intercom) for $3.6B in all-cash, folding the team and its support model into Agentforce. Close is expected by January 2027, Salesforce's fiscal Q4.

The agentic-CRM race is now being won by acquisition, not by shipping another feature. Fin is the latest in a run of roughly ten AI acquisitions Salesforce has made in the past year, and at $3.6B the largest of them except for its $8B Informatica deal. Salesforce did not buy a roadmap item. It bought a proven, domain-tuned model and the team that built it. That resets the build-versus-buy math for every platform competing on AI agents.

Here is the part that decides whether $3.6B was smart or expensive.

The model is the artifact everyone points to. It is not the asset. The asset is the judgment of the people who know why the model works, and that judgment never shows up on the balance sheet. Two questions will determine the return.

Does the acquired model keep its independence, or does it get diluted into the platform until the thing worth buying is gone?

Does the team that built it stay, contribute, and feel real ownership ninety days after close, or do they vest and leave?

Acquirers underwrite the technology and underestimate the integration. The deal closes in a quarter. The retention and roadmap work that protects the thesis takes far longer, and none of it makes the announcement.

If you are running the integration on a deal like this, the first ninety days are where the thesis holds or breaks. What is your plan to keep the people who actually built the thing you just bought?

This is the throughline of what I build with the Product in Acquisitions OS: keeping an acquired product team and roadmap coherent through the first 90 days, when the strategic logic of the deal collides with everything already on the board. More on it this week.
